If you've tried this before
Process Mining isn't new.
What has changed?

Process mining has been around for over a decade. It works well — within a single system, on relatively clean processes. Most large organisations have tried it, often inside an ERP, and seen real value at that scope.

The decisions that matter most don't live inside a single system.

Complex processes — such as AI deployment, cost reduction, supply chain agility — traverse multiple systems, platforms and people. The invisible work that happens between them — the workarounds, the delays, the human judgement calls — is what Process Mining alone cannot see.
