Your position
You're already making good decisions.
With the data you have.

No organisation reaches the scale yours has without smart people making considered decisions under pressure. The question isn't whether you're making the right calls — it's what the same calls would look like with a more complete picture.

Most organisations operate with visibility into individual systems. Very few have visibility across them — across the workflows that span departments, the spreadsheets that bridge systems, the workarounds that have become standard practice.

The opportunity isn't to make better decisions. It's to make the same kinds of decisions with significantly richer information underneath them.
If you've tried this before
If you've explored process intelligence before and didn't get what was promised, that experience is worth understanding.

Process mining has been around for over a decade. It works well — within a single system, on relatively clean processes. Most large organisations have tried it, often inside an ERP, and seen real value at that scope.

The decisions that matter most don't live inside a single system.

AI deployment, cost reduction, supply chain agility — these traverse multiple systems, multiple teams, and the invisible work that happens between them: the spreadsheets, the emails, the workarounds, the human judgement calls. Process mining alone cannot see that.

Simulation

Process Intelligence
The four layers of Process Intelligence.
Most large organisations have only explored layer one. The picture changes considerably when you have all four.
Layer 01Process Mining
Maps how a process runs, typically within a single system. Surfaces bottlenecks, variants, and rework loops. Proven and well-established — this is what most organisations have experienced.
Layer 02Task Mining★ Apromore's core strength
Captures the work that happens between systems and at the desktop level. Where the genuinely complex inefficiencies live — and what makes the next two layers meaningfully more accurate.
Deployed responsibly: process steps, not surveillance. Aligned with regulatory requirements and organisational values from day one.
Layer 03Analysis
Turns process maps into commercial reality. Quantifies the cost of inefficiency, the value of recoverable waste, the impact of every workaround. Richer data in — sharper answers out.
Layer 04Digital Twin / Simulation★ Apromore's core strength
Models the impact of a proposed change before it's implemented. Removes the gamble from significant transformation investments — AI rollouts, supply chain redesign, automation programmes.
Task mining and simulation are where the market has moved meaningfully in the last two years.
Understanding has value
The promise isn't that you'll become more efficient.
The promise is that you'll know.
Most organisations sit somewhere between two truths: confident they're running well, and uncertain how to prove it.
If you're already efficient
Process Intelligence lets you demonstrate it — to your board, your shareholders, your executive team — with forensic evidence rather than well-argued confidence. That's not a small thing in the current environment.
If you're not
It shows you exactly where the inefficiency lives, what it costs you, and in what order to address it. So you stop spending a million dollars to solve a ten-dollar problem — and start spending ten dollars to solve million-dollar ones.
Either outcome is a win. Both come from the same source: understanding what is actually happening inside your business.
That is the power knowledge gives you.
